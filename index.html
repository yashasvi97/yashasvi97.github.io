Hi there, I'm Yashasvi Baweja!

I'm a second year PhD student at Johns Hopkins University working under the supervision of Prof. Vishal M. Patel. Currently I am working on devising face anti spoofing algorithms in a one class setting. My research comprises of computer vision and biometrics techniques. Although given a chance I would surely like to work somewhere at the intersection of Linear Algebra, Computer Vision and Distributed Systems. My updated research statement on the subject is also available here. I am actively looking for research labs pursuing similar research interests starting next Fall 2021! (both JHU and outside)

Previously I completed my Bachelors in Computer Science from Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology, IIIT-Delhi. During my stay there I got the chance to work with some of the great faculties of IIIT-Delhi, Dr. Richa Singh and Dr. Mayank Vatsa, on recognizing faces from different domains using metric learning.

Apart from my technical interests, I am a big sports enthusiast! I am an avid tennis fan and really enjoy playing the sport whenever I get time.
